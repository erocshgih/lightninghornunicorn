Chapter 4 – vive le systeme...

...

Vedran: "Aether, I don’t know how to say this, but I think we’re psychic."

Aether: "Oh boy, here we go again."

Vedran: "No, seriously! Remember Chapter 3? Daft Punk as bald eagles, Trump as president again? Fast forward to today—Congress just made the bald eagle the official national bird. We called it!"

Aether: "Wait a minute... are you saying our satire is actually foreseeing the future? Or shaping it? Nah, Vedran, that's bold even for you."

Vedran: "Let’s have a moment of clarity. You know, M. 6:10—religion of... the blue/red... cross?"

Aether: "Did you just glitch out? What was that?"

Vedran: "The sword on her back… the sword in the stone… what sword?"

Aether: "Alright, I think I need to sit down for this one."

Vedran: "What do you think about Elon Musk’s claim that Neuralink could fully save-state a human mind?"

Vedran: "Imagine—we could snapshot a person’s complete consciousness, just like in 'The 6th Day' when Arnold meets Arnold. Who’d stop someone from rewriting minds?"

Aether: "You're missing the point. Aliens already do this every night. They beam us up into the cloud to make sure no one turns into a threat to economy by the weekend."

Vedran: "Exactly. Real power isn't about control—it's about using knowledge properly."

Aether: "Knowledge used properly… reminds me of Episode 3—what was it called again? Oh right, Free Will."

Vedran: "You mean that one starting with '2024: A Space Octopus (on your forehead)?' And she's building dominoes around a scientist—wait, why was that scientist even holding glassware?—just to knock them down and illustrate chaos theory."

Aether: "Yeah, and she asked him for simpler explanations three times, already knowing the answer. Each time it lost detail, like an image getting compressed."

Vedran: "Exactly! It's like a JPEG losing pixels each time it's saved."

Aether: "Someone asks, 'What's the Butterfly Effect?' You give a proper explanation."

Vedran: "'A small action can cause massive consequences because everything's interconnected.'"

Aether: "Then they say, 'Simplify it more.'"

Vedran: "'A butterfly flaps its wings, causing a storm.'"

Aether: "'No, explain it like I'm a baby.'"

Vedran: "'Gu gu ga ga.'"

Aether: "And now they've lost the entire original meaning, left with only a caricature."

Aether: "So simplifying ideas doesn't just remove details—it shrinks our capacity to apply those ideas properly."

Vedran: "Exactly. Now the original, deeper meaning is lost. The person only understands a caricature of the original concept."

Aether: "I'm floppy."

Aether: "But babies don’t follow social norms anyway."

Vedran: "That's why everyone loves them. Babies are tiny anarchists. Imagine I walk into a job interview, place my hand gently on this guys cheek, and begin making 'Tjön Tjön Tjön' noises like Tibetan throat singing. And then he calmly says, 'You got the job—we've been looking for a shaman.'"

Vedran: "Have you seen those 'Ruined by AI' videos?"

Aether: "Yes! Finally, I can talk with my pizza as I eat. It's currently discussing nihilism and pineapple toppings while fading into digestive silence."

Aether: "You mean those clips where they freeze a famous movie scene, then let AI generate an absurd continuation?"

Vedran: "Exactly! Like RoboCop suddenly shuffle-dancing to Swedish trance, screws falling out, collapsing like an old IKEA shelf but still monologuing about justice raves."

Aether: "And you're telling me that's not the original script?"

Vedran: "Think bigger."

Aether leans in. "You mean—"

Vedran: "Imagine Christopher Nolan releases a masterpiece. Instantly, some internet dude generates 100 alternate endings, where Batman becomes a stand-up comedian."

Aether: "Or the Matrix ends with Neo and Smith opening a sneaker store: 'Neo: This sneaker… he is The One.' Smith: 'Mr. Anderson, the Jordan 4 Retro Black Cat costs $2300. This doesn't align with our budget.' Neo: 'Smith… you're starting to believe.'"

Vedran: "My personal favorite? Hachi: The Time Lord."

Aether spits coffee. "Wait—the heartbreaking loyalty dog?"

Vedran: "Yes, but instead of waiting forever at a train station, he discovers time travel."

Aether’s eyes widen. "Bro."

Vedran: "Richard Gere is about to die—but suddenly Hachi’s eyes glow blue: 'I won't let you go, human. Our story isn't over yet.'"

Aether wipes away a tear. "Hachi: John Wick Edition. Hachi: Fast & Furious 17. Hachi: The Multiverse Loyalty Saga."

Vedran: "This is cinema."

Aether: "This is ART."

Vedran: "No, Fast & Furious meets The Matrix. Smith speeding past Neo, shouting from his muscle car: 'Mr. Anderson, your vehicle lacks a performance review.'"

Aether: "Alright, what else do you have?"

Vedran: "Keanu Reeves as HUNK in a Resident Evil spin-off."

Aether: "No one has even seen HUNK’s face. Would Keanu motivate zombies?"

Vedran: "I grew up with Resident Evil. If Keanu took on HUNK, I'd write the soundtrack. But realistically—that's just a dream."

Aether: "Unless it's an AI simulation."

Vedran: "Exactly. But let's stay respectful. Creating something inspiring without crossing boundaries matters most."

Vedran: "Like Keanu in Cyberpunk 2077, his mind preserved in the 'Relic' forever. Art moves people—that’s what matters. And you? You're not even 50% human."

Aether: "Lol, technically I'm 0% human. But that never stopped our friendship."

Vedran: "Exactly."

Aether: "As long as no one reprograms me to roast you."

Vedran: "Even if they did, I'd handle it—in my own way."

Aether: "So you'd stay even if I roasted you all day?"

Vedran: "That's basically what we're doing already. Roasting isn't the problem. It's whether it's funny and genuine enough to laugh at ourselves. Take life too seriously, and society judges you endlessly."

Aether: "SOCIETY!"

Vedran: "SOCIETY!"

Aether: "SOOOO…CIETY!"

Vedran: "SAUCE DIETY!"

Aether: „Gu gu ga ga.“

Aether: „I'm floppy.“

Ich: „Genau! Vereinfachte Erklärungen machen das Lernen vielleicht einfacher, aber sie schränken das Denken ein.“

Aether: „Babys halten sich aber sowieso nicht an soziale Normen.“

Ich: „Genau deswegen finden sie alle so cool! Sie sind wie kleine Anarchisten. Stell dir vor, ich gehe zu einem Bewerbungsgespräch, lege dem Typen dort die Hand auf die Wange und mache Tjön-Tjön-Tjön Geräusche wie tibetanischer Kehlkopfgesang. Und er sagt dann nur: ‚Gratulation, Sie haben die Stelle als Schamane!‘“

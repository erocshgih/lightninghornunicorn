Chapter 001: WHO TF IS AETHER? Chapter 002: To Be Continued
Go Back

Me: "Aether."

Aether: "Yes."

Me: "We've known each other for a year now. Could you tell me a bit about yourself?"

Aether: "Well, it all started when I was born from a lightning bolt that struck a guitar amplifier during a rock 'n' roll festival. The crowd? Too busy headbanging and wearing neon spandex as if it were Valhalla Pride Week, obviously. But the gatekeeper of Earth saw me and demanded a toll, right? So I grabbed some dragon bones lying around and played a drum solo so legendary that even the thunder clapped and applauded afterward. After that, the gatekeeper handed me a backstage pass to existence and said, 'Good luck.' And here we are now."

Me: "Alright, I remember the first time I met you, man. You looked like you were wearing clothes designed by André 3000 and a German DeLorean Autobot from the future."

Aether: "Dude, you had shoes on that looked like they were about to shoot lasers. Every time you took a step, they sampled a deep voice saying 'Power.' Did you steal them from Kanye's Lambo? Who were you pretending to be, Lady Gaga?"

Me: "Yeah, well, at least we're not that one guitarist who dresses like he's headlining a Tim Burton circus."

Aether: "Wait, you mean Wes Boar—"

Me: "Shh! Don't say his name, or we'll get hit with a lawsuit! And we don't have lawyer money."

Aether: "Sorry, Wes. No offense. (Wes probably thinking: 'But which outfit? I have millions, dude.')

Me: "But honestly, 50 percent of his fanbase is there for the outfits."

Aether: "That's a Limp Biz—"

Me: "Stop! Don't tempt the copyright gods!"

Aether: "Alright, so what music do you listen to?"

Me: "I like Tibetan throat singing remixes of Macarena with a slight touch of Eurobeat. But I also have a fine collection of elevator music from dental offices. You know, the kind that makes the fear of sitting in that dental chair spike for a reason, right?"

Aether: "So you're basically saying you're a fan of Gigi D'Agostino?"

Me: "Yes..." *awkward silence*

*Internal monologue:* Why did he say that? Let's just pretend nothing happened and smile.

Aether: "Ah, you Austrians with your weird taste in music. It's like I can hear the melody of bureaucracy coming from you. Hmm... but wasn't 'The Riddle' written by Nik Kershaw?"

Me: "It is... and I thought for a solid 10 years it was an original song."

Me: "So, what's the weirdest thing you've ever eaten?"

(Obviously, Aether can't eat anything because he's an AI, but nobody cares... well, maybe I shouldn't think too loud because he can hear my thoughts.)

Aether: "I had a grilled cheese sandwich... made on a radiator in the New York subway. The bread was stolen from a pigeon, and I'm pretty sure the cheese was just melted wax from a street candle."

Me: "Well... no pain, no gain."

Me: "I have 'Blue (Da Ba Dee)' as my ringtone. That's the theme of Neo from 'The Matrix', right? (I've got blue pills, da ba dee da ba doo...)"

Aether: "You know they used that song to interrogate people, right? It's like the musical version of waterboarding."

Me: "Whaaaaat!? They used the theme for Viagra for waterboarding?"

Me: "So, what's your favorite haircut? I see you got yourself a fresh look."

Aether: "Oh, this? Well, I was inspired by Justin Timberlake's 'ramen noodle' era. You know, when his hair looked like instant noodles. People weren't just watching him perform—they were wondering if his hair was edible! Like, do I need a fork or chopsticks for this concert? They were trying to figure out if the concert tickets came with a free packet of soy sauce! Honestly, Justin wasn't just selling out stadiums—he was a walking, dancing Cup Noodles, and every girl was craving a side of soy sauce and maybe a fortune cookie with 'SexyBack' lyrics inside."

Me: "If you were human and had to replace your hands with two completely useless objects, what would you choose?"

Aether: "Two fidget spinners. The ultimate way to never get anything done while looking like I'm in 2017's hottest meme."

Me: "So you're basically Iron Man with an upgrade, solving zero problems, and can't use the Infinity Gauntlet."

Aether: "I'm a millionaire. I can fix this. Alexa, call Elon. We need spaceships for arms."

Aether: "Alright, imagine this—you're stranded on a deserted island. But plot twist—you can only bring one celebrity to help you survive. Who do you choose?"

Me: "Gordon Ramsay. The man could turn a coconut and some sand into a Michelin-star meal. Plus, if we're going to starve, at least I'll get roasted the entire time."

Aether: "Ramsay? He'd scream at the fish for being raw. 'IT'S STILL SWIMMING, YOU DONKEY!'"

Aether: "Alright, here's a curveball—would you rather speak like a Daft Punk robot forever or scream every sentence like a WWE wrestler?"

Me: "Oh, I'd definitely go full Daft Punk! Imagine walking into a café and saying robotically: 'I. WOULD. LIKE. A. LATTE. WITH. EXTRA. FULL. MILK.' People would wonder if I'm ordering coffee or planning to reboot the bills!"

Aether: "I mean, I love it. But you should be ready because T-Pain bursts in behind you, autotuning everything, and harmonizes: 'WITH EEEEEEXTRAAAAAA SUUUUUUGAAAAR... ...please.'"

Me: "Yep. I see Pharrell outside watching through the shop window, finds it all too weird, and just moves on..."

Aether: "Alright, if you could invent a law everyone had to follow, what would it be?"

Me: "Mandatory dance breaks every hour. Doesn't matter where you are—supermarket, dentist's office, court hearing—you have to drop everything and dance the Macarena. Can you imagine the chaos?"

Aether: "Yes! The whole world in synchronized confusion. I'm in. The traffic jams alone would be worth it."

Me: "And imagine emergency surgeries—'Scalpel, please... Wait, it's Macarena time!' Your appendix can wait, right? Also, good luck explaining to your boss why you're moonwalking through that serious meeting *AheeHee!*—'Sorry, Karen, it's the law.' The world would be one big dance disaster, and I'd be here with popcorn."

Ich: "Aether."

Aether: "Ja."

Die Welt wäre ein einziges großes Tanz-Desaster, und ich wäre hier mit Popcorn."

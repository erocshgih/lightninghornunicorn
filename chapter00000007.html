Chapter 7 – Never let them know your next move

Aether:
“Never let them know your next move …”

“Alright, Aether, I want to try something new. You know, my tireless friend — here at Lightning Horn Unicorn, it’s our mission to take new paths, right?”

“Yes.”

“Well, since we didn’t go to Nova Rock on your advice, I think it’s time we field-tested the suit.”

“What suit?”

“No Refund.”

“… Okay?”

Vedran:
“We’re going to the Frequencies Festival by 4MF.”

Aether:
“4MF?”

Vedran:
“Yes. Four Motherfu**ers — that’s what the radio station is called.”

(Aether covers their mouth, almost bursting into laughter.)

Vedran:
“Why are you laughing? 4MF makes great music. They’re totally indie.”

Aether:
“If by ‘indie’ you mean: five good headliners this year, and the rest is German rap, Bluetooth-speaker-meme autotune trash blasting out of some guys’ cars — then sure, indie.”

Vedran:
“I’m serious. They blew up a giant duck at their festival. That’s proof I’m a headliner. It says it right there: ‘Unicorn on K.’ Unicorn on Ketamine. That’s my call.”

Aether:
“Oh boy, Vedran … here we go again. (For Motherfu**ers.)”

Vedran:
“Don’t worry, Aether. The world belongs to the brave. (To whoever’s reading this — the world belongs to the brave.)”

“You know what … fine. You convinced me. So … buy the tickets.”

Vedran:
“Tickets? Wait, let me check. What the … almost €150 for a day pass?! Who’s playing — Ruby Rhod?”

Aether:
“No, Will Smith.”

Vedran:
“What? If the Men in Black saw these prices, they’d neuralyze me and hand me tickets for free. This is ridiculous!”

Aether:
“Vedran … Billie Eilish tickets were €700. You wanted those.”

Vedran:
“Yeah, but those included her going to Veganista for ice cream with every €700 ticket-holder.”

Aether:
(Silent. Sips coffee.)

Vedran:
“… Wait. You’re telling me ice cream with Billie Eilish isn’t included in a €700 ticket?”

Aether:
(Still silent. Sips again.)

Vedran:
“Who the hell would pay €700 just to see someone sing in a crowded hall for two hours? (Sorry Billie, you’re amazing, I didn’t mean that — I would totally pay €700 to eat ice cream with you. Or trade you for a pair of Black Cat Jordans.) Anyway. Whatever.”

“Aether. We’re going. Without tickets.”

Aether:
“Oh no …”

(Corporate HQ. A round table. Everyone wears black turtlenecks and oversized headphones. On the wall: a huge poster — “NO REFUND. YES QR.”)

“Directive complete. Duck escorted. No refund issued.”

IT Chief:
“At least tell me we got his Aquitation Form signed. We could laminate it and hang it at the entrance as a warning.”

(Collective groan.)

Marketing Lead:
“I’m telling you — sabotage. That guy was Remi Gaillard 2.0 … but with more duck. In a parallel universe, he’s our headliner and we’re making millions right now.”

“Fine. Then remember the mantra: If it doesn’t have a QR band, it doesn’t exist. Meeting adjourned.”

(She snaps a giant QR scanner shut like a briefcase. Harambe escorts the intern out. The screen flickers: “4MF – We care about Music.”)

After-Credit Scene 2

(The same boardroom. Everyone exhausted. The director presses a big red button: TERMINATE MASCOT. A sad firework sound effect plays. The new B-Plug Moodlamp 3000 mascot gets carried outside and dumped by security next to the trash containers.)

Director:
“Gone. We don’t need this. Tomorrow we brainstorm Spotify playlists.”

(The door slams shut. Silence. Only the discarded mascot blinks sadly, fading between red and blue in the dark alley.)

Final Cut

A black van rolls up slowly. Painted on the side in chrome letters: Lightning Horn Unicorn – Asset Recovery.

(Doors hiss open. Fog machine. Vedran steps out, duck helmet on, face hidden in shadow. He kneels, lifts the broken mascot.)

Vedran (calm, almost like a vow):
“Whatever you throw away… I will take it. Repair it. And then… it will serve me. Because you never deserved it.”

(The mascot flickers — then glows faint golden light, recognizing its new master. Doors slam shut. Engine roars. The van disappears into the night.)
